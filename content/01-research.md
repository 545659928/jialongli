## Our Vision

We pursue research under the vision of **SHARE**: **S**oftware with **H**umanity, **A**daptability, and **RE**liability.

### Humanity

According to a global survey by KPMG, more than half of the respondents expressed concerns about AI controllability and privacy security. Our goal is to improve the user-friendliness of AI-driven autonomous software and support broader social acceptance.

Key questions include:

- How can we improve system transparency and user control?
- As system autonomy increases, how should systems communicate with users to build trust and reduce responsibility gaps?

### Adaptability

Modern software runs in increasingly dynamic and uncertain environments. We study how software can flexibly respond to change through self-adaptation, self-optimization, self-repair, and self-evolution.

Key questions include:

- How can we implement practical mechanisms for self-adaptation, optimization, repair, and evolution?
- How can we coordinate software requirements, architecture, and behavior when change propagates across them?

### Reliability

Reliable software begins with rigorous design, testing, and verification. We investigate how to make systems robust against attacks, noise, and unexpected conditions while keeping assurance affordable.

Key questions include:

- How can we design systems to be robust and resilient against attacks or noise?
- How can we efficiently verify correctness through decomposition and suitable analysis granularity?
- How can we find more bugs at lower cost during testing?

## Application Areas

The application domains we currently target include:

- Autonomous driving
- Robotics
- Video games
